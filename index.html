Definition

Water is a transparent, tasteless, odorless, and nearly colorless chemical substance that is the main constituent of Earth's streams, lakes, and oceans, and the fluids of most living organisms.

Google - Oxford Languages

Why water is important?

Water is important across all aspects in the world. From being an essential for the survival of all living organisms to regulating the climate, water is critical to the advancement, survival of our Earth.

Life

  • Building Blocks of Life
  • Ecosystem Support - Aquatic habitats, nourishes soil hence supports plant growth, drinking water
  • Human Survival - Human health, hygiene, sanitation, cooking, drinking, cleaning

    • Production

  • Agriculture and Food production - Irrigation and crop growth
  • Industrial production - Cooling, cleaning, and processing
  • Energy production - Hydropower, cooling plants etc
